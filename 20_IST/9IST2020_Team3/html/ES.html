Exams and Stress

Stress Less

Stress is a natural physiological response required to help you deal with challenging situations. Good amounts of stress can enhance your performance, particularly during exams. Extended periods of stress are not so good for you. Learning how to cope with different level of stress is an important way to look after your wellbeing.


What stresses you out?

Take some time to pinpoint what makes you feel stressed. During senior secondary school, common stressors include high workload, time constraints, exams, money problems, relationship issues and high expectations by teachers and parents. Change your habits

A healthy lifestyle can help you manage your stress levels. Any skill or strategy that makes you feel confident and puts you in control can help you manage your stress levels.

  • Eat nutritious meals and snacks and drink plenty of water .
  • Aim to sleep at least 6-8 hours each night.
  • Exercise regularly.
  • Find a balance between leisure, work and study.
  • Don't worry, it wastes valuable time and energy.
  • Learn relaxation techniques.
  • Don't compare yourself to others.
  • Form a support network to take about what is troubling you.
  • Seek help with personal issues.
  • Remember laughter is the best medicine.
  • Learn some time management and organisation skills.
  • Set SMART goals
  • Avoid overload by learning to say "no".
  • Be realistic about what you expect from yourself.


Take the Stress Test

Have you ever asked yourself how well you cope with stress? Do you often find yourself telling people how stressed you are? Take the stress test to find out whether you need to practice some stress management techniques.


The Stress Test

Using the key below, rate how often the following statements apply to you and place your score next to that statement.

1 = never

2 = rarely

3 = sometimes

4 = usually

5 = always

Example: If you never try to do as much as possible in the least amount of time, your rating for Statement 1 would be 1 = never.

Relaxation

Being a student can be tough. From falling out with friends to falling behind with homework or getting in trouble at home for staying out too late. The more that happens, the harder it is to concentrate, so sometimes the best thing to do is to take a step back and relax.


Our top 5 tips to relax

Click for more info!

Having fun with your friends and family is a great way to help you relax. They say laughter is the best medicine and it's true! Why not go for a pizza with your friends and have a laugh. You will be suprised how much better you feel.
Go to bed early enough to allow for at least eight hours sleep. Don't study in bed and put your phone on silent at night so that you are not being woken up by Facebook, Twitter and Instagram updates.
Listening to music is not only a great way of putting you in a good mood but it is also a great way of helping you relax. Did you know that classical music not only helps you relax but also helps you study better?
When you feel like things are getting on top of you, exercise is a great way to relax. Exercising helps your body produce endorphins, which make you feel good! Why not go for a run to clear your head or take a fitness class with a friend and see how much better you feel afterwards.
Sitting quietly for ten minutes a may to meditate can work wonders to help you relax. It's simple, just close your eyes and gradually relax by controlling your breathing. Count to three as you breathe in, and again as you breathe out. Repeat this step and least three times and relax.
