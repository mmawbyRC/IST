Time Management

Manage Your Time Well

Good time management is essential to achieving your goals at school this year. There are many strategies that will help you achieve more in less time. Here are some suggestions.


Multi-task: Do more than one thing at once

You will achieve more in the same amount of time. For example, revise your Science notes or read a novel for English on the bus ride home.


Plan: Make a commitment to tasks that lie ahead

Create a planner that clearly details your tasks and commitments for the week, month and year ahead. Display your planner where you will see it each day. Don't forget to include extra-curricular sports and activities as well as free time so as you have the right healthy balance between work and play.


Prioritise: Put things in order of importance

if you have a project due this Friday and an essay due the following Monday, work on both but spend more time on the project that is due first


When in doubt about what to do first - do something

If you hit the wall and can't thik of what to write (writer's block) just write whatever comes into your head for a while until your train of thought starts moving again.

Balancing Sport and Study

Being active is a great way to release endorphines and give your brain a rest from school and homework. Participating in sporting activities is also good for your wellbeing for a variety of reasons such as:

  • Reduces the risk of obesity
  • Increases your cardiovasular fitness
  • Improves your coordination and balance
  • Helps in the growth of your bones and muscles
  • Improves your sleep
  • Assists in confidence building
  • Teaches you the importance of leadership and cooperation

It is also important to remember that just because you participate in sporting activities this doesn't mean your grades will suffer. In fact sport is a great way to relax and re-energise your mind.


Our top 5 tips on how to juggle both sport and study

Make a timetable of when your sporting and study commitments are so that you can organise and delegate your time evenly.
At the start of each week make sure you organise your priorities. If you have a science test then delegate some extra time to study. Or if you have a sporting final, arrange a time for additional practice.
Use your travel time to school, practices or games to study where you could review your study notes or read your schoolbooks.
Use your weekends to catch up on anything you have fallen behind in so that you can start each week fresh and organised.
Be sure to get plenty of rest to re-energise your mind and body.
