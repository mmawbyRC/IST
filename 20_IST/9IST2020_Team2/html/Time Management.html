For
Time Management

Good time management is essential to achieving your goals at school. There are many strategies that will help to achieve more learning in less time. Some suggestions are listed below.

01

Multi-Task: Do more than one thing at once

You will achieve more in the same amount of time. For example, revise your science notes or read a novel for English on the bus ride home.

02

Plan: Make a commitment to tasks that lie ahead

Create a planner that clearly details your tasks and commitments for the week, month and year ahead. Display your planner where you will see it each day. Don't forget to include extracurricular sports and activities as well as free time so you have the right healthy balance between work and play. Make sure to keep regularly check and modify the scheduale as to make sure you don't forget anything. If you would like to download a planner, click on the template on the right.

03

Prioritise: Put things in order of importance

If you have a project due this Friday in an essay due the following Monday, work on both but spend more time on the project that is due first. Always prioritise the tasks due first, as to get them done quicker and you can focus on other tasks you have due.

04


Take regular rest and snack breaks

Rest revitalises your body and brain. Food gives your muscles and brain energy to burn. Although you are usually sitting when studying, you burn a tremendous number of calories when you are actively using your brain. Rest and feeding your brain keep you working at your best. If you like, you can take a crack at the duck game on the Home Page. But don't forget to go back to studying.

05

When in doubt about what to do first - do something

If you hit the wall and can't think of what to write (writers block) just write whatever comes into your head for a while until your training thoughts start moving again. This gives your brain time to regenerate and get into a studying focus. This technique also allows your to brainstorm many ideas about whatever you are working on, expanding your knowledge on the subject.
